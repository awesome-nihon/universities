## Application Resources

### Examination Systems

#### EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission)
- Required for most undergraduate admissions
- Offered twice yearly (June and November)
- Available in Japanese and English
- [Official EJU Website](https://www.jasso.go.jp/en/eju/index.html)

#### Individual University Exams
- Each university conducts its own entrance examination
- May include written tests, interviews, and portfolio reviews
- Requirements vary by program and university

### Application Portals
- [JASSO Gateway to Study in Japan](https://www.studyinjapan.go.jp/en/)
- [Japan Study Support](https://www.jpss.jp/en/)
- [JREC-IN Portal](https://jrecin.jst.go.jp/seek/SeekTop?ln=1) - For research positions

### Requirements Checklist
- [ ] Valid passport
- [ ] Academic transcripts
- [ ] Certificate of graduation
- [ ] Language proficiency (JLPT or TOEFL/IELTS)
- [ ] EJU scores (if required)
- [ ] Statement of purpose
- [ ] Letters of recommendation
- [ ] Financial proof
- [ ] Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

## Scholarships

### Government Scholarships

#### MEXT Scholarship (Monbukagakusho)
- **Full scholarship** covering tuition, living expenses, and airfare
- Available for undergraduate, graduate, and research students
- Highly competitive
- [Official MEXT Information](https://www.studyinjapan.go.jp/en/planning/scholarship/)

### University Scholarships

#### Major Scholarship Programs
- **University of Tokyo Fellowship (UTF)** - For graduate students
- **Keio University Scholarships** - Multiple programs
- **Waseda University Scholarships** - Various undergraduate and graduate scholarships
- **Kyoto University Scholarships** - Research and study scholarships

### Private Scholarships
- **JASSO Scholarships** - Various support programs
- **Rotary Yoneyama Memorial Foundation**
- **Otsuka Toshimi Scholarship Foundation**
- **Mitsubishi UFJ Trust Scholarship Foundation**
- **Asian Development Bank-Japan Scholarship Program**

## Student Life

### Accommodation
- **University Dormitories** - Most affordable, limited availability
- **Share Houses** - Popular among international students
- **Private Apartments** - More expensive but more independent
- **Homestay** - Cultural immersion opportunity

### Part-Time Work
- International students can work up to **28 hours per week** (40 hours during breaks)
- Common jobs: Teaching English, convenience store, restaurant
- Requires permission to engage in activities other than studies

### Student Organizations
- Circle activities („Çµ„Éº„ÇØ„É´) - Sports, culture, hobbies
- International student associations
- Academic clubs and research groups

### Health Insurance
- National Health Insurance (NHI) required for all residents
- Covers 70% of medical expenses
- Monthly premium around ¬•2,000-3,000 for students

## Language Schools

For those who need to improve Japanese language skills before university:

### Top Language Schools
- **[KCP International Japanese Language School](https://www.kcpinternational.com/)** - Tokyo
- **[ISI Japanese Language School](https://www.isi-global.com/)** - Multiple locations
- **[Yoshida Institute of Japanese Language](https://www.jpschool.ac.jp/)** - Tokyo
- **[Kyoto Japanese Language School](https://www.kyotojls.co.jp/)** - Kyoto
- **[Fukuoka Foreign Language College](https://www.fflc.ac.jp/)** - Fukuoka

### JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test)
- N5 (Beginner) to N1 (Advanced)
- N2 typically required for undergraduate programs
- N1 often required for graduate programs in Japanese
- Held twice yearly (July and December)
